JamaicanFood: Authentic Food Delivery & Recipe Box Builder

A zero-to-one iOS application bringing authentic Jamaican ingredients and culinary culture to the diaspora spanning discovery, recipe-based box building, e-commerce checkout, fulfillment, and post-delivery engagement.

Context

JamaicanFood is an independent, zero-to-one product venture built to test a specific and underserved market gap. The Jamaican diaspora across the US, Canada, and the UK, more than three million people, has no dedicated, trustworthy digital channel for sourcing authentic specialty ingredients. The recipe-box model has already been proven commercially by HelloFresh, Factor, Blue Apron, Green Chef et al; but no one has applied it to a culturally specific cuisine with this combination of high purchase intent and low competition.

This case study captures the opening phase of a four-stage effort that runs from product strategy and wireframe validation, through high-fidelity design, into beta launch, and finally scale across subscription and new markets. The work shown here is the strategy and design foundation: the problem framing, the end-to-end journey, the key product decisions, and the eleven core screens that prove the concept before any production code is written.

Where this fits: everything that follows is the groundwork that de-risks the build. Getting the journey and the box-builder model right at this stage is what makes the later launch and scale phases viable rather than speculative.

The problem

Jamaican diaspora communities and international food enthusiasts lack a reliable, culturally authentic digital channel to source Jamaican specialty ingredients. Mainstream grocery platforms either don't carry these products or carry unreliable, lower-quality substitutes. Sourcing staples like Walkerswood Jerk Seasoning, Grace Ackee, or Scotch Bonnet pepper sauce involves significant friction and there is no recipe-to-cart workflow for Jamaican cuisine anywhere.

The opportunity: a 3M+ Jamaican diaspora across the US, Canada, and UK, growing global interest in Caribbean cuisine, an underserved niche with high purchase intent, and a recipe-box model already proven viable by HelloFresh, Factor, Blue Apron, Green Chef et al.

The core product insight: a dual-mode ordering experience, shop by recipe (the app auto-populates a box with required ingredients) or shop by ingredient (the app suggests dishes you can cook with what you've selected).

Vision & goals

To be the most trusted digital marketplace for authentic Jamaican food, making it possible for anyone, anywhere, to cook and enjoy Jamaican cuisine with the right ingredients and cultural context.

  • Enable the diaspora and food enthusiasts to source authentic ingredients online with confidence.
  • Reduce the cognitive load of Jamaican meal planning through a recipe-to-ingredients box builder.
  • Build cultural connection through recipe context, origin stories, and traditional cooking tips.
  • Support multi-currency purchasing (USD, CAD, JMD, GBP, EUR) for domestic and international markets.
  • Deliver a frictionless mobile checkout anchored by Apple Pay.

Primary users

  • Segment 1: Jamaican diaspora (US, Canada, UK) seeking authentic ingredients unavailable locally.
  • Segment 2: Food enthusiasts and home cooks exploring Caribbean cuisine for the first time.
  • Segment 3: Second-generation Jamaicans reconnecting with cultural cuisine and family recipes.

Key product decisions

1 · Dual box-building modes

Research into comparable services (HelloFresh, Green Chef) showed a single path works for homogeneous audiences. Jamaican food buyers have two distinct mental models: cooks who start from a recipe, and pantry-builders who stock staples and figure out meals later. Offering both Recipe-Based and Ingredient-Based modes, plus Quick Start boxes for new users maximizes user match without adding backend complexity.

2 · Recipe gap analysis in ingredient mode

The Ingredient-Based builder includes a post-selection gap analysis panel ("Ackee & Saltfish: missing saltfish") showing which recipes are achievable and what's missing.

Why it works: the panel drives upsell by informing rather than promoting. Users complete their pantry naturally, increasing average order value without aggressive merchandising or a dedicated recommendation engine in v1.

3 · Apple Pay as primary payment

Apple Pay is the primary CTA at checkout, not an equal option alongside saved cards. For an audience that may be skeptical about entering payment details on a new marketplace, it provides the trust and security framing of the Apple ecosystem, reduces checkout time, and aligns with the iOS-first platform strategy.

4 · Multi-currency at checkout, not as a global setting

Currency selection (USD, CAD, JMD, GBP, EUR) lives at the checkout stage, defaulting to USD with one-tap switching. This reduces v1 implementation complexity while still signaling international reach; full multi-currency settlement is scoped to v2.

5 · Cultural context as a feature, not filler

Cultural notes on recipes, occasion notes, origin stories, and cooking tips are scoped as a first-class feature. For diaspora users they're an authenticity signal; for new-to-cuisine users they're the educational on-ramp that drives confidence and repeat purchase. This differentiates the product from generic grocery delivery and creates a content flywheel for retention.

Screens & design system

The wireframe set covers eleven screens across the full journey: Onboarding, Home / Browse, Box Builder Entry, Recipe Selection, Box Preview, Shopping Cart, Checkout, Order Confirmed, Order Tracking, Recipe Detail, and Ingredient-Based Box. Each was designed to iPhone portrait dimensions (375×812pt) with Dynamic Type support, VoiceOver labels, high contrast support, and 44pt minimum touch targets per Apple HIG (Human Interface Guidelines) and WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines). The full high-fidelity set is shown below, grouped by the five journey stages.

The palette starts from the Jamaican flag's green and gold, then deepens and warms both for screen legibility: a forest green (#2a473c) as primary and a muted gold (#c59e4b) as accent, grounded by a warm ink (#172721) over cream surfaces. The full set carries national identity through every screen while staying easy on the eyes across long browse sessions.

Other notable screen-level decisions: recipe boxes appear in the cart as bundled line items to preserve the mental model built in the box builder; the promo code field is visible in the cart to reduce abandonment; and the USPS tracking view uses a milestone timeline designed to reduce WISMO ("where is my order") support contacts.

Validation plan

  • 5–7 moderated usability sessions with diaspora users before development handoff.
  • A/B test Recipe-Based vs. Ingredient-Based entry point prominence on the home screen.
  • Soft launch in Brooklyn, The Bronx, and Queens before nationwide rollout.
  • Track WISMO rate weekly post-launch; a 10% threshold triggers fulfillment review.
